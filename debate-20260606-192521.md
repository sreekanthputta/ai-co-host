## 3. Riskiest technical part and how I’d de-risk it

### Riskiest part

**End-to-end real-time audio → retrieval → reasoning → private whisper under live-demo pressure.**

The real failure mode is not Moss. It is:

- ASR misses “2019 assault conviction”
- diarization mislabels the speaker
- Minimax generates too much text
- TTS starts too late
- the whisper collides with the defender speaking
- you spend 16 hours debugging audio plumbing

### De-risk

Do **not** attempt open-room diarization.

Use LiveKit correctly:

- Prosecutor has their own mic/track.
- Judge has their own mic/track.
- Defender has a receive-only “earpiece” track.
- You know which audio stream is prosecutor because it is a separate participant.
- No ambient courtroom recording.
- No hard diarization problem.

Use a constrained pipeline:

1. LiveKit captures prosecutor audio.
2. Streaming STT produces partial transcript.
3. Trigger only on phrases like:
   - “prior conviction”
   - “failure to appear”
   - “request bail”
   - “danger to the community”
   - “no ties to community”
4. Moss retrieves from **case packet only**.
5. Minimax receives:
   - prosecutor utterance
   - top Moss snippets
   - tiny curated legal checklist
6. Minimax outputs max 25 words for whisper.
7. LiveKit plays private TTS to defender.

Also: have a fallback button.

If live ASR breaks, teammate hits “inject prosecutor line,” and the rest of the pipeline still runs. The audience still sees LiveKit, Moss, and Minimax working. Don’t let one audio bug kill the whole demo.
